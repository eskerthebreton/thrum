## Accessible Design

**Circumstance**: You make a Struction gambit to create a ramp, scaffolding, or
similar structure to facilitate entry to a building or structure
at a spot not normally accessible.
**Effort Cost**: 1
**effect**: The Gambit is Controlled and Powerful
**Upgrades:**


## Hate if Something Happened To It

**Circumstance**: You or an ally make a gambit (using any Art) to intimidate someone
**Uses/Cooldown**: 1/Episode
**Effect**: Treat the outcome as one category better than the roll
(Critical Fumble becomes normal Fumble, Fumble becomes normal Success,
Success becomes Critical Success)  
**Upgrades:**


## More Like a Window

**Effect**: You always have **+1 Boon** on Gambits to destroy obstacles
(such as doors)  
**Upgrades:**


## An Eye for Workmanship

**Effect**: You can glean the following pieces of information about the visible parts
of a building or other structure without a roll:\n
  * Any structural weak-points\n
  * The approximate age of the construction, as well as whether any parts were added or replaced more recently\n
  * The approximate thickness of any wall, floor, or ceiling, and any hollowed out or similarly altered segments
**Upgrades:**


## Fixer-Upper

**Success**: Treat the quality of your downtime accommodations as one tier higher.
If it is already a luxury accommodation, the party (re)gains 1 Eunoia.
**Special**: This activity should be resolved before allies' downtime activities,
as it affects their rolls
**Upgrades:**


## No Place Like It

**Effect**: When you select this ability, create a clock of 4, 6, or 8 segments,
representing your project constructing a base of operations for the party.
Each time you succeed on this activity but at most once per arc, mark off
one segment. When the clock is complete, the base is usable for downtime
with quality corresponding to the clock size (4 for modest, 6 for standard,
8 for luxury), but must be maintained at a rate corresponding to the cost of
downtime at its quality tier once per arc. Additionally, each time it is
used, roll Struction with Opposition equal to the number of times the party
has used the dwelling this arc. On a failed roll something breaks and the
base cannot be used again until it is repaired by taking this downtime
activity during downtime spent in other accommodations.
